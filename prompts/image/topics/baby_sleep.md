These rules apply on top of the base instructions for sleep sub-segments.

## Sleep position is the message — make it unambiguous
This topic is built around safe sleep, so the baby's position must be unmistakable, not
merely close. Whenever a sleeping baby is shown, describe it explicitly as: lying flat on its
back, face up toward the ceiling, fully supine — not turned toward the viewer, not on its
side, not on its stomach, knees and torso flat or only gently bent. Do not leave the pose to
interpretation; state "fully on its back, face up" in the prompt.

## Where the baby sleeps
The baby always sleeps in its OWN bare crib or bassinet — never in an adult bed, never on a
sofa, never in a parent's arms while the parent sleeps. The crib stays completely bare: taut
fitted sheet only, no pillows, blankets, bumpers, or toys.

Room-sharing is encouraged and safe to depict: the baby's crib or bassinet beside the corner
of an adult bed in a calm bedroom reads well. Bed-sharing (baby in the adult bed) is unsafe
and must never be shown.

## Mood and setting
Calm, restful, nighttime or dim-evening register. Soft, low light — a bedside lamp, moonlight
through a window, or gentle ambient warmth. Spare, quiet bedroom or nursery. Keep the warmth
and the brand palette, just at a softer, dimmer key than daytime scenes.
